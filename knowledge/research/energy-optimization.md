# Energy & Optimization Research Reference

## Schwartz — 4 Energy Dimensions

| Dimension | What It Is | Dan's Application | Minimum Fix |
|-----------|-----------|-------------------|-------------|
| Physical | Sleep, nutrition, exercise, recovery | Mid-day crash = physical energy debt. Skipping lunch is depleting the battery. | Eat by 12:30. Non-negotiable. |
| Emotional | Relationships, confidence, patience | Every interaction as CEO is a transaction. Needs refueling before creative peak. | 10-min decompression between work mode and creative mode ~9 PM |
| Mental | Focus, creativity, time management | Peak is 9:30 PM-2 AM. Gets 2 genuine deep-work blocks (ultradian 90-120 min cycles). | Protect 9:30-11:30 PM ruthlessly. Second block 11:45-1:15 AM. |
| Spiritual | Purpose, values alignment | "What did I do this week that only I should be doing?" | Weekly 15-min review |

**Key insight:** Energy is managed in oscillations (stress + recovery), not straight lines. Working continuously without recovery creates compound energy debt.

## Circadian Science for Night Owls

| Finding | Application |
|---------|-------------|
| Chronotype ~50% genetic | Stop trying to become a 6 AM person. Optimize around it. |
| Morning light (10K lux) shifts clock | Get outside within 30 min of waking. Can shift 30-60 min earlier over weeks. |
| Meal timing entrains peripheral clocks | 3 meals at consistent times daily. Irregular meals desynchronize and cause crashes. |
| Temperature signals sleep | Hot shower 60-90 min before bed → rebound cooling → faster sleep onset. |
| Social jetlag (weekend shift) | Keep sleep/wake within 1-hr window, 7 days/week. |

## Minimum Effective Fitness

| Finding | Evidence | Application |
|---------|----------|-------------|
| 150 min/week = most cognitive benefit | Meta-analyses (Erickson, Hillman) | 2x swim (45 min) + 2x tennis (60 min) = 210 min. Above threshold. Enough. |
| Acute exercise boosts cognition 2-4 hrs | Chang et al., 2012 | Exercise 5-7 PM → cognitive boost bridges to 9:30 PM creative peak |
| Tennis > solo swimming for mental health | Chekroud, 2018 (1.2M participants) | Social element is a mental health multiplier. Keep both, prioritize tennis if cutting one. |
| 15 min bodyweight 2x/week | Liu-Ambrose et al. | Easy add. Pushups, squats, planks. Before shower. No gym. |

## Sleep Optimization

| Lever | Mechanism | Implementation |
|-------|-----------|---------------|
| Consistency (#1 lever) | Regular times stabilize circadian alignment | Same time ±30 min every day. 6 hrs consistent > 7.5 hrs random. |
| NSDR | Restores dopamine ~65%, partially compensates for short sleep | 10-min "Huberman NSDR" on YouTube at 3-4 PM energy dip |
| Sleep efficiency | Time asleep / time in bed | Get in bed only when sleepy. If not asleep in 20 min, get up. |
| Caffeine cutoff | Half-life 5-6 hrs. Reduces deep sleep 15-20% even when you fall asleep fine. | Last caffeine 8:30 PM for 2:30 AM sleep |
| Target | 5-6 hrs is a real deficit. Only ~1% have short-sleep gene. | Push to 6.5 hrs minimum. Either sleep earlier or wake later by 30 min. |

## Self-Tracking That Works

| Finding | Application |
|---------|-------------|
| Tracking alone changes nothing | Data without reflection = noise. Most tracker users abandon in 6 months. |
| Subjective 1-10 energy > wearables | Simple self-rating 3x/day builds body awareness. More actionable. |
| 2-week experiment framework | Treat changes as experiments with success criteria. Prevents "forever commitment" resistance. |
| Weekly 10-min review > daily tracking | Diminishing returns on granularity. Weekly pattern review is the sweet spot. |

## Behavioral Reframing (Sutherland)

| Reframe | Example |
|---------|---------|
| Loss → Gain | "I have to eat lunch" → "Lunch fuels my creative peak" |
| Obligation → Identity | "I should swim" → "I'm someone who moves through water" |
| Restriction → Luxury | "Can't work past 2 AM" → "Hard stop protects my best asset" |
| Failure → Data | "Missed 3 swims" → "Learned Thursday evenings don't work" |
| Discipline → Design | "Need more willpower" → "Need fewer decisions" |
