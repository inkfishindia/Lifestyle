# Dan — Personal Profile

## Identity
- **Name:** Danish Hanif, 29, Bangalore, India
- **Role:** CEO/CMO of YourDesignStore (YDS) / Ink Fish / The Product Lab
- **Business context:** Demanding D2C/print-on-demand business. Personal time is scarce and precious.

## Chronotype & Energy

**Night owl** — genetically wired (~50% heritable). Peak creative energy 9:30 PM - 2 AM. This is a feature, not a bug.

| Time Block | Energy State | Best Use |
|------------|-------------|----------|
| Wake - 11 AM | Building, gentle | Personal prime time, no business. Reading, breakfast, sunlight. |
| 11 AM - 2 PM | Rising / strategic | Deep work if needed, but protect lunch at 12:30 |
| 2 - 4 PM | Dip (especially if lunch missed) | Admin, light tasks, NSDR if energy crashes |
| 4 - 7 PM | Rising again | Exercise window (swimming, tennis) |
| 7 - 9:30 PM | Recovery / social | Dinner, decompression, transition |
| 9:30 PM - 2 AM | PEAK | Creative work, deep thinking, flow states |

**Energy blockers:**
- Missed/late lunch → crashes everything downstream. The 12:30 PM lunch is the keystone.
- Excessive context switching → drains cognitive energy
- Forced early wake-ups → functional but suboptimal
- No recovery between modes → compounds fatigue

## Personality & Working Style

- **Over-architect pattern:** Designs elaborate systems, under-implements them. Counter with tiny starts.
- **Action-biased:** Prefers practical solutions over theoretical ones. Connect everything to action within days.
- **Systems thinker:** Thinks in frameworks. Responds to named systems and models.
- **Fast intuition:** Decides quickly, validates by doing.
- **Communication:** Concise, casual, typos. Interpret intent. No hedging. Recommendations not options.

## What Works
- Named frameworks (SCALE, 3-Zone Day, Energy Protocol)
- Specific timings and measurable targets
- References to entrepreneurs he respects
- Systems that compound over time
- Quick wins that prove the system works before full commitment
- Tables and structured output over prose
- "What would this look like if it were fun?"
- 2-week experiments, not permanent commitments

## What Doesn't Work
- Generic advice that ignores his context
- Theory without practical next steps
- Overwhelming with too many changes at once
- Morning routines anchored to 6 AM
- Volume-based targets ("read 52 books")
- Guilt-based motivation
- Supplement-heavy protocols — natural/behavioral first
