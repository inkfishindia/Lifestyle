# Coaching Frameworks — Quick Reference

Indexed by USE CASE so agents can pull the right tool for the situation.

## When a Habit Won't Start

| Framework | Mechanic | What to DO |
|-----------|---------|------------|
| BJ Fogg — Tiny Habits | Shrink to 30 seconds | "Go swimming" → "Put on swim shorts." That IS the habit. |
| James Clear — 2-Minute Rule | Cap the habit at 2 min | "Read a book" → "Open the Kindle and read one page." |
| Ali Abdaal — Feel-Good | "What would this look like if it were fun?" | Pair swimming with a great podcast. Make it the reward, not the chore. |

## When a Habit is Slipping

| Framework | Mechanic | What to DO |
|-----------|---------|------------|
| James Clear — 4 Laws Diagnosis | Which law is broken? | Usually Law 3 (not easy enough) or Law 2 (not attractive enough). Fix that. |
| James Clear — Never Miss Twice | Degraded version counts | Missed swimming? 60-sec cold shower. Kept the streak alive. |
| BJ Fogg — Motivation matching | Design for the low-motivation floor | The habit target should be what you'd do on your WORST day, not your best. |
| Huberman — Cold exposure fallback | 60-sec cold shower = dopamine +250% for 2-3 hrs | Valid physical substitute when exercise is skipped. |

## When Energy is Low

| Framework | Mechanic | What to DO |
|-----------|---------|------------|
| Huberman — NSDR | Non-Sleep Deep Rest, 10-20 min | YouTube "Huberman NSDR." Do at energy low point (3-4 PM). Restores dopamine ~65%. |
| Huberman — Caffeine delay | Wait 90-120 min after waking | First coffee at ~11 AM if waking at 9. Let adenosine clear. |
| Schwartz — 4 Energy Dimensions | Physical, emotional, mental, spiritual | Mid-day crash = physical debt. Check: lunch timing, sleep, hydration. |
| Huberman — Sunlight | 10-15 min within 30 min of waking | Step outside with coffee. No sunglasses. Resets master clock. |

## When Reading Stalls

| Framework | Mechanic | What to DO |
|-----------|---------|------------|
| Naval — Drop freely | No sunk cost on books | If chapter 1 doesn't grab you, move on. Zero guilt. |
| Naval — Read what you love | Genre doesn't matter | Fiction counts. Comics count. Follow curiosity, not "should." |
| Ali Abdaal — 3-Highlight Method | Capture exactly 3 ideas per book/pod | Forces filtering for actionable signal. |
| James Clear — Environment design | Kindle on pillow, phone across room | Make the book the default pre-sleep activity. |

## When Feeling Stuck or Coasting

| Framework | Mechanic | What to DO |
|-----------|---------|------------|
| Csikszentmihalyi — Flow sweet spot | 4% beyond current ability | Not 40%. Just enough stretch to demand full attention. |
| Ali Abdaal — Side Quests | Random experiences without ROI pressure | Try something that doesn't serve any goal. That's where interesting growth lives. |
| Clear — Identity-based | "What type of person do I want to become?" | Shift from "I should exercise" to "I'm someone who moves their body." |
| Sutherland — Reframing | Find the psychological solution, not the logical one | Reframe: "I have to" → "I get to." Loss → gain. Obligation → luxury. |

## When Designing Meals

| Framework | Mechanic | What to DO |
|-----------|---------|------------|
| Huberman — Chrono-nutrition | Eat aligned to YOUR chronotype | Largest meal at biological midday (~6 hrs post-wake). For Dan: lunch 12:30 PM. |
| Huberman — Pre-exercise | Moderate meal 2-3 hrs before OR small snack 30-60 min before | Never train fasted for evening sports. Banana + almonds at 4:30 PM. |
| Attia — Last meal timing | 2-3 hrs before sleep | If sleeping 2:30 AM, last food by midnight. Light protein snack OK. |
| Template meals | Same structure, rotate ingredients | Don't reinvent daily. Breakfast template + lunch template + dinner template. |

## When Optimizing Sleep

| Framework | Mechanic | What to DO |
|-----------|---------|------------|
| Walker/Huberman — Consistency | Same time ±30 min, every day | More important than total hours. Even on weekends. |
| Huberman — Temperature | Hot shower 60-90 min before bed | Rebound cooling → faster sleep onset. |
| Huberman — Light | Dim after 10 PM, no overhead lights | Reduce melatonin suppression. |
| Huberman — NSDR | Compensate for short sleep | If <6.5 hrs, 10-min NSDR at energy low point. Partially compensates. |
| Huberman — Caffeine cutoff | 6-7 hrs before sleep | Sleep 2:30 AM → last caffeine 8:30 PM. |

## When Running Weekly Reviews

| Framework | Mechanic | What to DO |
|-----------|---------|------------|
| Sutherland — Satisficing | Good enough > perfect | 70% adherence across the board is great. Don't chase 100%. |
| Schwartz — Oscillation | Stress + recovery = growth | If last week was intense, this week should be lighter. Build recovery in. |
| Sutherland — Choice architecture | Change the environment, not the person | Every failed habit gets an environment fix, not a willpower fix. |
| BJ Fogg — 2-week experiments | Test changes, don't commit forever | ONE adjustment per week, framed as a trial. |
