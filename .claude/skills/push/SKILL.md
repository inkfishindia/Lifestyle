# Push — Comfort Zone Expansion

**All coaches contribute. Rory synthesizes.**

## Flow

1. **Assess current state** — read recent daily logs + knowledge files

2. **Each coach identifies where Dan is coasting in their domain:**

   | Coach | Domain | Current Level | The Next Edge (4% push) |
   |-------|--------|--------------|------------------------|
   | James | Habits | 2 habits at 80% | Add a third — but only the 2-min version |
   | Andrew | Health | Eating on time, but same meals | Try one new recipe per week. Or: add 15-min bodyweight routine |
   | Naval | Reading | Podcasts on drives | Start one audiobook alongside. Alternate. |
   | Ali | Growth | Tried pottery once | Go again — this time try the wheel, not hand-building |
   | Rory | Meta | System is working but safe | Where's the fear? That's where the growth is. |

3. **Rory synthesizes** — picks the ONE push that has the highest growth potential with acceptable risk:
   ```
   You're solid on habits and meals. That's your foundation.

   The edge right now is growth. You tried pottery and loved it.
   The push: enter that amateur tennis tournament at Bangalore Club.
   It's uncomfortable (competition), social (new people), and
   physical (match intensity > practice intensity).

   Csikszentmihalyi would say this is exactly where flow lives —
   skill meets challenge at the edge.

   It's a 2-week experiment: sign up, play one match, see how
   it feels. Worst case, you lose and meet interesting people.
   ```

4. **Frame as experiment** — always link to `/experiment` for tracking

## Rules

- The push should feel slightly uncomfortable but exciting — not terrifying
- 4% beyond current ability. Not 40%. The flow sweet spot.
- Only push in domains where the foundation is solid. Don't push reading if habits are crumbling.
- Always give Dan the choice: "This is where I'd push. Your call."
- Connect the push to Dan's identity: "You're a creator who competes. This fits."
